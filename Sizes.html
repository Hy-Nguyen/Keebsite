Keyboard Sizes

The most common form of keyboards are TKL, Ten Key Less, or a full sized, which contains the numpad on the right side. However many peple enter the hobby in search for a smaller form factor, such as a 60 or 65 percent keyboard. Some common form factors are:

  • 60%
  • 65%
  • 75%
  • TKL
  • 1800/Full Sized
  • 40%
  • Alice

Each of these are different in form factor, but choosing between them is up to preference and use case. For example, the 60 and 65 percent form factor do not utilize the function keys, but the 75 is the same as the 65, but with the function row. So depending on your workflow, you might find yourself needing the function row, or perhps you would be fine without it.
